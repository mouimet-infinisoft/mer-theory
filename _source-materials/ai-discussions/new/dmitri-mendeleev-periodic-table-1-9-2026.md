The Breakthrough: The Periodic Table (1869)
Mendeleev's stroke of genius was recognizing that elements, when arranged by atomic weight, showed periodic repetition of properties.
His Method
Legend has it that Mendeleev wrote each known element on a card with its properties and atomic weight, then played a kind of "chemical solitaire" - arranging and rearranging them until patterns emerged. What he discovered was revolutionary:

Elements with similar properties appeared at regular intervals (periodically)
Rows and columns created families - elements in the same column behaved similarly
The pattern predicted gaps - where elements should exist but hadn't been discovered yet

The Boldest Move
Here's what made Mendeleev truly brilliant: he left gaps in his table for undiscovered elements and predicted their properties with stunning accuracy.
